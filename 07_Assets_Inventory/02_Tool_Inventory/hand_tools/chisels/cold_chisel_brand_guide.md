## Top Tier Brands (Daily Professional Use)

### Dasco Pro
- **Your 408 3/4" Example**: Excellent balance of price, performance, and availability.
- **Strengths**: American-made, tough high-carbon steel, great for electrical and fleet work.
- **Best For**: Everyday jobsite cutting, shearing bolts, concrete cleanup.

### Mayhew
- **Reputation**: Often considered the gold standard for punches and chisels.
- **Strengths**: Superior edge retention, heavy-duty construction. Many premium brands rebrand Mayhew steel.
- **Best For**: High-volume demolition and metal work.

### Klein Tools
- **Electrician Favorite**: Sized and balanced specifically for tradesmen.
- **Strengths**: Reliable, good control in tight spaces, widely available.
- **Best For**: Panel work, conduit straps, box installs.

### Proto / Wilde
- **Industrial Grade**: Heavy-duty American-made tools built for mechanics and contractors.
- **Strengths**: Exceptional durability under repeated hammer blows.

## Solid Mid-Tier Brands (Great Value)

- **IRWIN** — Forged one-piece, popular electrician cold chisels.
- **TEKTON** — Surprisingly good steel and finish for the price.
- **SK / Craftsman** — Solid Western Forge quality, readily available.
- **GearWrench** — Modern designs with good ergonomics.
- **Stanley** — Affordable sets, suitable for apprentices and backup tools.

## Premium / Specialty

- **Starrett** — Toolmaker precision and toughness.
- **Snap-on / Mac** — Lifetime tools (usually Mayhew-based).
- **Mayhew Pro / Enderes** — Step-up American made options.

## Budget / Occasional Use

- **Harbor Freight (Pittsburgh / Quinn)** — Acceptable for light or one-time jobs.
- **Amazon Basics / Generic** — Keep as emergency backups only.

## Recommendation for Electrical Contractors & Fleet Inventory

**Core Set Suggestion**:
1. Dasco Pro 408 (3/4") — Your current chisel
2. Dasco or Klein 1/2" cold chisel
3. 1" or wider chisel for heavier work
4. One Mayhew punch/chisel set for tough jobs

**Buying Advice**:
- Prioritize American-made (Dasco, Mayhew, Klein, Proto, Wilde) for tools you swing daily.
- Avoid super-cheap imports for regular hammer use — they mushroom fast and can become dangerous.
- Look for heat-treated high-carbon steel with precision-ground edges.
